📋 THE SCRIPT v1.3 — ASK before you pitch (tap to collapse)

Open: "Hey, is this the owner? — Nate, I'm local here in central Arkansas. Quick one before anything else — when somebody searches '[trade] near [town]' on Google right now, do you know if you show up, or does it go to somebody else?" (Let them answer. Don't skip this — it's the whole fix.)

Then: "Yeah, that tracks — most [trade] shops your size are in the same boat. So when someone new can't find you or finds nothing telling them how to book, where do you think they go?" (Let THEM say "the next guy." Now it's their conclusion, not your pitch.) "Here's why I called — I already built you a real site, no charge to look. Can I text it to you?"

Talk it up (not "a website"): "This is your whole online front door — a real site that's yours, your Google listing fixed so you show up on the map, and a small AI assistant that answers questions and books people when you can't pick up."

The offer: $250 one-time for the whole system. Then $79/month — hosting, updates, monthly Google refresh. Cancel anytime. "I build these myself so I only take a handful of new ones a week — yours is already built though."

Risk-killer close: "You don't pay a dime until it's live and you love it. You keep the site and domain either way." Live in 2–3 days. Then go SILENT — first to talk again loses.

Objections: "Too busy" → "That's the point — it runs while you work; I do all of it." · "I have Facebook" → "Great for regulars, invisible to someone new Googling you tonight." · "How much again?" → "$250 once, $79/mo, and nothing until you love it." · "Send me info" → "Doing it now — what's the best number to text the demo to?" · "Not interested" → ONE qualifier before you fold: "Totally fair — is it bad timing, or just not interested in being found online at all?" Real no after that → thank them, leave warm, done.

Tonality: slow down 20%, end statements flat (not a question lilt), quiet + calm on the close. Stripe: buy.stripe.com/dRm28rgjtfaq4wjfOfd7q04 · Log each outcome in CALL_OUTCOMES.md.
